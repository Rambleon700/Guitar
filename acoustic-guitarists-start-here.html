Getting Started / Welcome

Acoustic Guitarists: Start Here

Build strength, dexterity, and confidence from day one

Why acoustic guitar?

Acoustic guitars force your hands to work harder than electrics. The strings are thicker, the action is higher, and the payoff is huge: stronger fingers, better calluses, and a richer tone. If you can nail acoustic rhythm, electric feels like flying.

What you'll learn in this course

  • Open chord foundations: Em, Am, G, D, A, E and their inversions.
  • Strumming patterns: Down-up rhythms, muted hits, and fingerpicking basics.
  • Transition speed: Drills to move between chords without silence.
  • Real songs: Use everything together in actual material.
  • Ear training: Play what you hear and understand why it works.

Tip

Calluses take time. If your fingertips hurt in week one, that's normal. By week three, the soreness fades and your fingers toughen up. Keep a light practice schedule (15–30 min daily) and you'll progress fast.

Before you start

  • Make sure your guitar is in tune. Use a free tuner app or get your guitar professionally set up.
  • Sit upright. Poor posture kills motivation and causes hand pain.
  • Warm up your hands for 2–3 minutes before you play. Wrist rotations and finger stretches help.

Your first chords

We start with Em (E minor), Am (A minor), and G (G major). These three chords appear in thousands of songs.
